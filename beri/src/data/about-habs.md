## Vision and Values
### Core Statements
**Vision:** To be at the forefront of education
**Mission:** Two leading schools on one co-educational campus for ambitious and curious students; offering outstanding education that is fun, exciting and intellectually challenging, developing independence, resilience and a moral compass to create positive change.
**Purpose:** To empower young people to make a profound impact in the world.
**Source:** [Our Vision](https://www.habselstree.org.uk/boys/about-us/our-vision/)
### Four Core Values
#### 1. AMBITIOUS
Bold and ambitious for students, staff, and the world. Underpinned by philanthropic foundations. Students stretch boundaries, challenge status quo. When combined with courage, curiosity and community, ambition becomes a selfless lever for change.
#### 2. CURIOUS
Engage with the world by questioning, challenging assumptions, expressing ideas. Curriculum encourages imagination, creativity, spirituality, bringing awe and wonder to learning.
#### 3. COURAGEOUS
Explore beyond comfort zones to become self-aware, adventurous, resilient, independent. Cope well with setbacks. Balance humility and boldness. Unflinching problem-solvers seeking to change the world.
#### 4. COMMUNITY
One caring, respectful community extending to families, alumni, partner schools, Haberdashers' Company, and the world. Focus on belonging, inclusivity, celebrating differences. Learning from and working with communities locally, nationally, globally.
**Source:** [Our Vision](https://www.habselstree.org.uk/boys/about-us/our-vision/)
### Habs Student Profile
Ambitious, globally curious, tolerant, resilient, compassionate, kind, altruistic. Orchestrates compelling conversations. Initiates change. Strong moral compass. Determined to make a difference. Community-focused. **#ProfoundImpact**
**Source:** [Our Vision](https://www.habselstree.org.uk/boys/about-us/our-vision/)

## Senior School (Years 7-11, Ages 11-16)
### Overview
Ambitious, challenging, exciting curriculum. Expert teachers stretch students while sharing subject passion. Active, independent learning with practical tasks, discussion, debate. Students encouraged to take risks, investigate, explore.
**Expected to excel across:** Languages, sciences, maths, athletics, music, performing arts, technology, history, arts.
**Source:** [Senior School - Inside the Classroom](https://www.habselstree.org.uk/boys/senior/inside-the-classroom/)
---
### Curriculum Structure
Not bound by National Curriculum but stays current with developments. Ambitious, innovative, broad, balanced - all subjects equally important.
#### By Year Group
**Year 7:**
- French, Spanish, German carousel
- Choose 2 languages in Year 8
**Years 7-9:**
- Set but flexible curriculum
**Higher years:**
- Greater personalisation to suit needs/interests
- Up to 4 optional GCSE subjects
**Throughout:**
- Extended research projects (Family History Y7, academic papers later)
**Additional opportunities:**
- National/international competitions (Maths/Science olympiads)
- External speakers
- Academic societies
**Source:** [Senior School - Inside the Classroom](https://www.habselstree.org.uk/boys/senior/inside-the-classroom/)
---
### Transitions
**Lower School** (Y7-8) → **Middle School** (Y9-11) → **Sixth Form**
Bespoke one-to-one guidance at each stage, especially for GCSE/A-Level choices. Mid-senior school, staff begin helping students think about interests, university, career ambitions. Close monitoring of academic progress and wellbeing enables partnerships with parents for right decisions.
**Source:** [Senior School - Inside the Classroom](https://www.habselstree.org.uk/boys/senior/inside-the-classroom/)
---
### GCSE Results
Challenge students beyond exam curricula. Excel in exams and co-curricular/partnership commitments. Broad curriculum develops personal skills/attributes.
Single-sex education to GCSEs, benefiting from co-ed setting.

|-------|------------|
| **Grade 9** | 53% |
| **Grades 9-8** (A* equivalent) | 78% |
| **Grades 9-7** (A*-A equivalent) | 91.5% |
**Leavers' Destinations:** High proportion of students progress to Russell Group universities and Oxbridge.
**Historical results 2016-2025** available as PDFs on website.
